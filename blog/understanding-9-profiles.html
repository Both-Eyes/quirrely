Writing Fundamentals

Understanding the 9 Writing Profiles

Every writer falls somewhere in a matrix of tone and stance.

Quirrely analyses your writing across two dimensions: tone and stance. Tone describes the energy and texture of your prose. Stance describes your relationship with the reader — open and inviting, or closed and conclusive. Together they produce one of nine distinct profiles.

The 9 Profiles

Assertive

Clear positions, active verbs, confident claims.

Poetic

Imagery, rhythm, resonance. Ideas arrive through sensation.

Minimal

Economy above all. Every word earns its place.

Dense

Layered, complex sentences. Rewards rereading.

Conversational

Warm, direct, colloquial. Like a smart friend explaining clearly.

Hedged

Careful qualifications. Shows the limits of what can be known.

Interrogative

Questions as structure. Invites thinking rather than passive receiving.

Longform

Extended development of ideas. Comfort with complexity.

Contradictory

Holds tension. Argues against itself. Finds truth in paradox.

Why It Matters

When you know your profile you can make deliberate choices: lean into your natural voice, or consciously stretch toward a different mode. Reading taste and writing voice often align — but the gap between how you write and how you love to read is one of the most interesting things to discover.

How Profiles Are Detected

Quirrely analyses sentence structure, vocabulary density, punctuation patterns, and rhetorical markers. It does not judge quality — it identifies pattern. A Dense + Closed writer is not better or worse than a Conversational + Open one. Different instruments, different music.
