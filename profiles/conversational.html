The Conversational Writing Style

You write the way you talk. And that's exactly right.

What is Conversational Writing?

Conversational writers create prose that feels like a chat with a friend. Their writing is warm, approachable, and often includes the natural rhythms of speech—contractions, rhetorical questions, and the occasional tangent that somehow makes everything better.

This style builds connection. Readers feel like they know you. The barrier between writer and reader dissolves, creating intimacy and trust.

Example of Conversational Writing

"Look, I'm not saying I'm a great cook. I'm really not. But here's the thing—I made this pasta last night and it was, honestly? Kind of incredible. Like, I surprised myself. My roommate asked for the recipe and I had to admit I just threw stuff in a pan and hoped for the best. That's cooking, right?"

See how it flows? The asides, the self-deprecation, the rhetorical question at the end. It's writing that breathes.

Tips for Conversational Writers

  • Your warmth is disarming—use it to tackle tough topics
  • In formal contexts, you can dial back without losing yourself
  • Contractions are your friend (see what I did there?)
  • Read your work aloud—if it sounds natural, you're golden
  • Don't let editors strip all the personality out

Best Contexts for Conversational Writing

  • Personal blogs and newsletters
  • Social media and community building
  • Memoir and personal essays
  • Startup and brand content
  • Podcasts and scripts
