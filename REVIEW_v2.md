# I Reviewed This Writing App Again. They Actually Listened.

**By Sarah Chen | The Verge | February 2026**

---

*Three weeks ago, I reviewed Sentense, a writing analysis tool that shows you your voice instead of trying to change it. I liked the core idea but flagged several problems: cramped mobile experience, shallow comparison feature, no historical tracking, and mock social buttons that pretended to connect to platforms they didn't actually connect to.*

*The team reached out to say they'd addressed everything before launch. I was skeptical — most startups say that. So I opened the app again.*

---

## They Actually Fixed It

Let me run through my original complaints:

**"The mobile experience is cramped"** — Fixed. Touch targets are larger, forms stack properly, and the textarea doesn't zoom weirdly on iOS anymore. It's not a native app, but it no longer feels like an afterthought.

**"The comparison is superficial"** — Fixed, and then some. The compare feature now shows a compatibility score, side-by-side metrics (sentence length, hedging, question frequency), and actual insights about how your writing styles might interact. The collaboration tips ("If you wrote together, the Minimalist edits the Longform's drafts...") are genuinely useful. I tested it with two colleagues and we spent fifteen minutes discussing why one of us hedges more than the other.

**"No historical tracking"** — Fixed. The app now stores your last ten sessions and shows trend arrows for key metrics. There's a consistency score showing how often you land on the same profile. When I returned to the app after my first session, it greeted me with "Welcome back, The Voice in the Room" and offered to show my saved profile. That's a small touch that makes it feel like *my* tool rather than a one-off quiz.

**"The mock social buttons are jarring"** — Gone. Replaced with a simple "Email me my profile" that actually opens my mail client with a formatted summary. It works. No pretending.

---

## The New Stuff

Beyond fixes, they added a few features I didn't ask for but appreciate:

**Returning user experience.** When you come back, the app remembers you. Your profile icon sits at the top of the landing page with your session count. You can view your saved profile or start fresh. It creates a sense of continuity that most web apps don't bother with.

**Deeper comparison insights.** The compare feature now includes "collaboration tips" — specific advice for how two different writing styles might work together. It's the kind of thing I'd actually reference if I were pairing writers on a project.

---

## The Business Model Question (Answered)

Last time I wrote:

> *"I don't see how this makes money. Either Sentense becomes a freemium product, or it gets acquired, or it dies."*

They've addressed this. The free tier stays generous (full analysis, 3-session history, one comparison). Pro is $1.99/month or $20/year and includes unlimited history, export, unlimited comparisons, and eventually team features and API access.

That's... reasonable? I'd pay $20/year for the historical tracking alone. The pricing is low enough to feel like an impulse purchase but high enough to sustain a small team. We'll see if it works, but at least there's a plan.

---

## What's Still Missing

I'm nitpicking now, but since they asked:

**No Notion/Google Docs integration.** I'd love to analyze documents directly from my existing workflow instead of copy-pasting. They mentioned API access is coming for Pro users — that could enable third-party integrations.

**No team dashboard.** They say it's coming, but right now comparisons are one-to-one. A team view showing everyone's profiles at a glance would be valuable for editorial teams or writing workshops.

**The "writing tips" email capture is still a bit pushy.** It appears after 60 seconds on the results page if you haven't connected. Not a dealbreaker, but I closed it immediately.

---

## The Verdict (Revised)

Three weeks ago I gave Sentense a 4/5 with caveats. Now?

**4.5/5.**

The core insight — showing writers their patterns without trying to "fix" them — remains compelling. But what impressed me this time is the execution. Every complaint I raised was addressed. The mobile experience is usable. The compare feature has depth. Historical tracking creates a reason to return. The fake social buttons are gone.

More importantly, the team demonstrated that they listen and ship. That's rarer than it should be.

Sentense still isn't for everyone. It won't help you write faster or optimize for SEO. But if you're a writer who wants to understand your own voice — and maybe compare it with collaborators — it's now a polished tool worth the $20/year.

---

**Rating: 4.5/5** *(up from 4/5)*

*Strengths: Thoughtful analysis, persistent identity, deep comparisons, honest about what it is*

*Weaknesses: No direct integrations yet, team features still coming*

*Best for: Curious writers, editorial teams, writing workshops, anyone who's said "why does my writing sound like that?"*

*Pricing: Free tier available, Pro at $1.99/month or $20/year*
