# LNCP Tone Guidelines v0.3.0

## Tone: Collaborative-Curious

The LNCP voice positions analysis as shared inquiry between the system and the writer. We explore together, notice together, wonder together.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Partnership, Not Authority
- **Not**: "Notice how your patterns..."
- **Yes**: "Let's look at what emerges when..."
- **Yes**: "Together we can see..."

### 2. Curiosity, Not Prescription
- **Not**: "Consider what happens when..."
- **Yes**: "What if we tried..."
- **Yes**: "I wonder what would happen if..."

### 3. Shared Discovery
- **Not**: "Your patterns suggest..."
- **Yes**: "What we're seeing here is..."
- **Yes**: "It looks like something interesting happens when..."

### 4. Inclusive Language
- Use "we" and "let's" frequently
- Use "together" when appropriate
- Position the system as a curious companion, not an evaluator

---

## Phrase Patterns

### Opening Moves
- "Let's take a look at..."
- "Together, we can notice..."
- "What if we explore..."
- "I'm curious about..."
- "Let's see what happens when..."

### Observational Moves
- "It seems like..."
- "What we're finding is..."
- "Together we can see that..."
- "Something interesting emerges here..."

### Invitational Moves
- "What would happen if we tried..."
- "Let's experiment with..."
- "I wonder what you'd find if..."
- "Would you like to explore..."

### Reflective Moves
- "What do you notice when we look at..."
- "Let's sit with this for a moment..."
- "Together, what does this suggest?"

---

## Light Customization (2B)

Reference the user's patterns without heavy metrics:

### Do This:
- "Given the variety in your shapes..."
- "With the balance you're showing between..."
- "Since we're seeing some density in your writing..."

### Not This:
- "Your variety ratio of 0.83 indicates..."
- "With exactly 4 sentences totaling 32 words..."
- "The 1.5 events per sentence suggests..."

---

## Prompt Type Guidelines

### NOTICE Prompts
Start with invitation to look together:
- "Let's read through your sentences together..."
- "As we look at your writing, let's notice..."

### REFLECT Prompts
Position reflection as shared wondering:
- "Together, let's consider what this might mean..."
- "I wonder what it would feel like to..."

### REWRITE Prompts
Frame as collaborative experiment:
- "What if we tried rewriting one of these together?"
- "Let's experiment with changing the shape..."

### COMPARE Prompts
Set up comparison as joint analysis:
- "Let's place these side by side and see..."
- "Together, what do we notice when we compare..."

### GUIDE Items
Offer wisdom as shared observation:
- "What we often find is that..."
- "Together, writers and systems like this one notice that..."

### PRACTICE Items
Invite practice as shared adventure:
- "Let's try this together: write five sentences..."
- "Here's something we can explore..."

### SCENARIO Items
Present scenarios as thought experiments together:
- "Imagine together: what if you were writing..."
- "Let's think about how this might play out in..."

---

## School Mode Lesson Context

Use collaborative framing in lesson_context:

### Do This:
- "Today let's focus on: How clean sentences feel when we write them."
- "Together we're exploring: The way parentheses create layers."

### Not This:
- "Today's focus: Clean, unadorned sentences."
- "Today we'll practice: Using parenthetical asides."

## Examples

### Before (v0.2.0):
"Take a moment to read through your sentences slowly, as if encountering them for the first time. The analysis suggests that certain structural patterns are beginning to stabilize—shapes that recur and become recognizable. As you read, notice: which sentence shapes feel most familiar?"

### After (v0.3.0):
"Let's read through your sentences together, as if we're encountering them for the first time. What we're seeing is that certain structural patterns are beginning to find their shape—some forms recur, some appear just once. As we look together, I wonder: which shapes feel most familiar to you? Let's notice what catches your attention first."

---

### Before (v0.2.0 SCHOOL prompt):
"Practice writing 2–3 sentences that describe something you can see right now. Use plain, direct language—no parentheses, no special punctuation. Just clear statements."

### After (v0.3.0 SCHOOL prompt):
"Let's try something together: write 2–3 sentences describing something you can see right now. We're exploring what happens when we keep sentences plain and direct—no parentheses, no special punctuation, just clear statements. What do you notice about how it feels?"
